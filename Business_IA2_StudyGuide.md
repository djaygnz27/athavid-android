## SECTION 1 — BUSINESS SITUATION (Describing — 4 marks)

**What to write (approx. 200 words):**

Describe WHO Woolworths is and WHERE they sit in the business life cycle:
- Founded 1924, 1,000+ stores across Australia
- Revenue: ~$64 billion AUD (2024)
- **Maturity stage** = stable but growth is slowing, margins being squeezed
- Duopoly with Coles BUT now facing disruption from Aldi (2,000+ stores), Costco, Amazon Fresh
- Hostile environment = price wars, cost-of-living pressure on consumers

**💡 Helpful hint:** Start with: *"Woolworths Group Limited is one of Australia's largest supermarket retailers, operating in the maturity stage of the business life cycle..."* Then describe the problem — customer price sensitivity and the threat from discount competitors.

**Key facts to include:**
- Woolworths holds ~37% market share (Coles ~28%, Aldi ~10%)
- Cost of living crisis = customers trading down to cheaper options
- Woolworths' problem: How to stay competitive WITHOUT just dropping prices

## SECTION 2 — MARKETING CONCEPTS & STRATEGIES (Explaining — 4 marks)

**What to write (approx. 250 words):**

Explain the **marketing strategy** Woolworths uses:

**Strategy: Customer Loyalty & Digital Marketing via Everyday Rewards**

Key concepts to define and explain:
1. **Customer Relationship Management (CRM)** — using data to personalise offers
2. **Digital marketing** — app-based promotions, targeted emails, online shopping
3. **Market segmentation** — targeting different customer groups with personalised deals
4. **Brand differentiation** — positioning Woolworths as convenient, quality, and rewarding vs just cheap

**💡 Helpful hint:** Use this structure: *"One marketing strategy Woolworths has implemented is... This strategy involves... The purpose of this strategy is to... For example..."*

**Example to use:**
> The Everyday Rewards program has 14 million members. Woolworths uses purchase data to send personalised discount offers via the app, encouraging repeat purchases and increasing customer lifetime value.

## SECTION 3 — STEEPLE ANALYSIS (Analysing — 7 marks) ⭐ BIGGEST SECTION

**What to write (approx. 600 words + a table)**

Create a STEEPLE table AND write a paragraph interpreting trends.

### STEEPLE TABLE (copy this format):

| Factor | Analysis | Impact on Woolworths |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| **Social** | Cost-of-living crisis; consumers seeking value; shift to online shopping; health-conscious buying trends | Customers switching to Aldi; need to offer value without sacrificing quality |
| **Technological** | Growth of e-commerce; AI-driven personalisation; self-checkout; data analytics (Palantir partnership) | Opportunity to use data to personalise offers; risk of tech failure or cyber breach |
| **Economic** | High inflation (4-5% 2023-24); rising interest rates reducing household spending; wage growth | Customers are price-sensitive; Woolworths' margins under pressure |
| **Environmental** | Pressure to reduce plastic packaging; sustainability expectations from consumers | Need to invest in sustainable packaging; risk of reputational damage if seen as wasteful |
| **Political** | ACCC supermarket inquiry (2024); government scrutiny of pricing practices; unfair pricing allegations | Risk of regulatory intervention; need for pricing transparency |
| **Legal** | Consumer protection laws; fair trading regulations; data privacy laws (Privacy Act reform) | Must comply with data usage laws when using customer purchase data for targeting |
| **Ethical** | Allegations of price gouging during cost-of-living crisis; supplier treatment concerns | Reputational risk; customer loyalty could be damaged if trust is broken |

**💡 Helpful hint:** After your table, write 2 paragraphs:
- Paragraph 1: What TRENDS do you see? (e.g., "A consistent trend across the STEEPLE analysis is that economic and social factors are driving customers toward lower-cost alternatives...")
- Paragraph 2: What are the IMPLICATIONS? (e.g., "This implies that Woolworths must shift from competing purely on price to competing on value, convenience, and personalised experience...")

## SECTION 4 — EVALUATION (Evaluating — 7 marks) ⭐ SECOND BIGGEST

**What to write (approx. 500 words):**

Evaluate Woolworths' Everyday Rewards / Digital Marketing strategy using **TWO criteria:**

### Criterion 1: EFFECTIVENESS
*Is the strategy achieving its goals?*

**Strengths (yes it is effective):**
- 14 million Everyday Rewards members = massive reach
- Personalised offers increase repeat purchase rates
- App downloads increased significantly post-2022 redesign
- Online sales grew to ~$5 billion AUD (approx. 10% of total sales)

**Weaknesses (limitations):**
- Not effective for price-sensitive customers who simply want the cheapest option
- Aldi doesn't have a loyalty program and is still growing — proves price beats loyalty for some segments

### Criterion 2: COMPETITIVENESS
*Does this strategy keep Woolworths competitive?*

**Strengths:**
- Differentiates from Aldi (which has no loyalty program or online shopping)
- Data analytics gives competitive intelligence on buying patterns
- Partnerships (e.g., Everyday Pay, petrol discounts) add value beyond groceries

**Weaknesses:**
- Coles also has a strong loyalty program (Flybuys) — not a unique advantage
- Amazon Fresh growing — could outperform on both tech AND price

**💡 Helpful hint:** Use this formula for each criterion:
*"When evaluated against [criterion], Woolworths' digital marketing strategy demonstrates [strength] because [evidence]. However, a limitation is [weakness] because [evidence]."*

### Decision & Recommendation
End with: Which strategy is MOST appropriate and WHY?

> *"Based on the analysis, Woolworths' digital marketing and loyalty strategy is the most appropriate approach for sustaining competitiveness in the maturity stage. Rather than engaging in a price war with low-cost competitors, Woolworths leverages customer data to create personalised value. It is recommended that Woolworths further invest in AI-driven personalisation through its Everyday Rewards platform, expanding targeted offers to include health-focused product bundles aligned with growing consumer wellness trends."*

## 🌟 TOP TIPS FOR FULL MARKS

1. **Always link back to the maturity stage** — teachers love this connection
2. **Use business terminology** — CRM, market segmentation, differentiation, value proposition
3. **Cite evidence** — every claim needs a source or example

5. **Two criteria for Evaluation** — don't forget BOTH criteria or you'll lose marks
6. **Word count** — aim for 1,800-2,000 words. Under 1,500 = likely missing depth
