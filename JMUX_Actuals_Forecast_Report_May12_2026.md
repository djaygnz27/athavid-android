SECTION 1 — PAR CIRCUIT MIGRATION (PHASE 2)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

DESCRIPTION:
PAR (Phase Angle Regulator) circuits control high-voltage power flow at 6 critical substations. The legacy JMUX system carried these signals via direct serial connection. The new Nokia MPLS network is IP-based — requiring physical serial-to-IP conversion devices (Iniven RC-30) at each site plus full engineering design and relay labor for installation.

This scope was originally a Phase 3 placeholder estimated at $30K–$40K in 2024. The full scope was discovered during Phase 2 site surveys — it is mandatory for NERC-CIP compliance. There is no alternative path.

SITES AFFECTED (6):
1. Northport — also requires C37.94 protocol card (unique requirement)
2. Hicksville (5HK)
3. Melville
4. Lindenhurst (7Z)
5. Far Rockaway (2H)
6. Hewlett (2R)

SECTION 2 — PHASE 3 ENGINEERING PULLED INTO PHASE 2
═══════════════════════════════════════════════

DESCRIPTION:
To avoid supply chain delays and compress the overall project
schedule, Phase 3 site surveys and engineering (IFR/IFC
packages) are being started during Phase 2 staging (Aug 2026)
rather than waiting for Phase 2 completion.

WHY WE ARE ACCELERATING:
Nokia MPLS nodes have an 8–12 week lead time. If Phase 3
engineering starts after Phase 2 completes, equipment
delivery delays push Phase 3 construction into 2028.
Starting Phase 3 design in parallel with Phase 2 staging
compresses the schedule by 4–6 months and avoids double
mobilization costs for Hawkeye construction crews.

Confirmed by Ethan (B&M): "As soon as Phase 2 staging
begins, Phase 3 engineering can start."
