## SLIDE 2 — Goals & Objectives
**[As you advance to Slide 2]**

"The purpose of this project is to modernize PSEG Long Island's substation communications by replacing the legacy GE Jungle MUX system with a new Nokia MPLS private network — delivering a reliable, scalable, and future-ready infrastructure.

Our four core workstreams are:

**Engineering** — Burns & McDonnell handles all site surveys, rack elevations, power and cabling design, and produces the IFR, IFC, and as-built documents.

**Procurement and Staging** — we're coordinating across Burns & McDonnell, Nokia, and Graybar for equipment ordering, bill of materials, and staging.

**Construction and Installation** — Hawkeye handles all physical installation per approved IFC documents. Burns & McDonnell provides on-site technical oversight and commissioning support.

**Deployment and Application Migration** — this is where PSEG and Burns & McDonnell configure the Layer 3 networks, pre-stage application connectivity, and execute the production migration.

In terms of scope — we're looking at 129 substations total across three phases, 74 MPLS sites, and 79 Nokia MPLS nodes when fully complete."

## CLOSING — Q&A Prep

**If asked: "Why is SPI at 0.76 and not recovering faster?"**
"The delays were administrative — SSP security review queue and CMDB coordination. The physical and technical work has been executing well. BGP approval and execution by April 2nd is the recovery event."

**If asked: "Are we at risk of budget overrun?"**
"We are tracking within the $6.6M approved budget. We are monitoring Phase 2 labor costs closely — specifically the PAR controls field labor estimate — and will have a firmer number at the next update."

**If asked: "When does JMUX get decommissioned?"**
"Our earliest consideration for JMUX decommissioning is April 2026 for Phase 1 sites. We will not touch JMUX until Nokia is fully validated and remote access is confirmed."

**If asked: "What is the PAR controls work?"**
"PAR stands for Phase Angle Regulator — these control power flow between transmission circuits. We are integrating PAR controls into the Nokia network using new INIVEA interface devices. Pilgrim is our first beta site, with delivery expected in April. This work runs in parallel to the main JMUX replacement and does not impact our primary schedule."

**If asked: "Who handles Nokia after Burns & McDonnell leaves?"**
"Operations and maintenance of the Nokia system will transfer to Vic's team — our internal IT group including Rahiq and Brianna. Nokia training is actively being scheduled to ensure a smooth handover."
